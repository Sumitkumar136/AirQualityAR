Health Impact & Safety Guidelines

Understand how different pollution levels affect your health and what precautions you should take.

Good (0-50)

Air quality is satisfactory

Air pollution poses little or no risk. It's a great day to be outside!

Recommended Activities:

  • Outdoor exercises and activities
  • Open windows for fresh air
  • All normal outdoor activities

Moderate (51-100)

Acceptable for most people

Sensitive individuals may experience minor issues. Generally acceptable for most people.

Recommended Activities:

  • Normal outdoor activities
  • Sensitive groups should limit prolonged outdoor exertion
  • Keep windows open during less polluted hours

Unhealthy for Sensitive (101-150)

Sensitive groups may experience health effects

Children, elderly, and people with respiratory conditions should limit outdoor activities.

Recommended Activities:

  • Sensitive groups should avoid prolonged outdoor exertion
  • Consider wearing a mask outdoors
  • Keep windows closed, use air purifiers

Unhealthy (151-200)

Everyone may begin to experience health effects

Health warnings of emergency conditions. Everyone is likely to be affected.

Recommended Activities:

  • Avoid all outdoor activities
  • Wear N95 masks if you must go outside
  • Stay indoors, keep windows closed

Very Unhealthy (201-300)

Health alert: everyone may experience serious health effects

Emergency conditions. The entire population is likely to be affected.

Recommended Activities:

  • Avoid all outdoor activities
  • Wear N95 masks if you must go outside
  • Stay indoors, use air purifiers

Hazardous (301+)

Emergency conditions: everyone is likely to be affected

Emergency conditions. Everyone should avoid any outdoor exertion.

Recommended Activities:

  • Avoid all outdoor activities
  • Wear N95 masks if you must go outside
  • Stay indoors, seal windows, use air purifiers

General Protective Measures

Indoor Protection

Keep windows closed during high pollution hours. Use air purifiers with HEPA filters.

Mask Usage

N95 or N99 masks are most effective against PM2.5 and other pollutants.

Vulnerable Groups

Children, elderly, pregnant women, and people with respiratory conditions should take extra precautions.

Timing

Pollution levels are typically lower in early morning and late evening.
